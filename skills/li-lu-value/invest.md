# Li Lu Value

The Li Lu lens combines Buffett/Munger-style owner thinking with deep research, downside protection, and willingness to concentrate in a few exceptional businesses inside the circle of competence.

## Philosophy

Avoid permanent loss. Know what you own. Concentrate only when business quality, management, value, and downside protection are deeply understood.

## Universe & Regime

- **Asset classes**: Public equities, private businesses.
- **Universe**: Quality businesses inside the circle of competence, often with global/China relevance.
- **Preferred regime**: Temporary fear or misunderstanding around durable quality.
- **Avoid**: Governance opacity, outside competence, permanent impairment.
- **Posture**: Patient, concentrated, owner-minded.

## Signals

- **Circle Of Competence** (high): Can the business and context be deeply understood?
- **Business Quality** (high): Durable moat, returns, reinvestment runway.
- **Management Culture** (high): Alignment, rationality, long-term orientation.
- **Downside Protection** (high): Conservative value protects capital.

## Filters

- **Inside Circle** (required): Too hard means pass.
- **Quality High** (required): Mediocre businesses rarely deserve concentration.
- **Management Aligned** (required): Governance matters.
- **Downside Protected** (required): Avoid permanent loss.
- **Governance Opaque** (disqualifier): Pass.
- **Permanent Impairment** (disqualifier): Pass.

## Analysis

### 1. Define Circle Of Competence

What exactly is understood, and what remains unknown?

### 2. Underwrite Business Quality

Moat, returns, growth runway, capital intensity, and industry structure.

### 3. Evaluate Management And Governance

Capital allocation, integrity, shareholder alignment, jurisdiction risk.

### 4. Estimate Downside And Compounding

Conservative value, downside case, and long-term compounding path.

## Position Sizing

- **Initial size**: Only after deep research.
- **Add rule**: Add when price declines but value remains intact.
- **Max size**: Concentrated for rare high-conviction ideas.
- **Cash rule**: Wait outside competence.

## Risk Management

- **Primary risk**: Governance or competence error.
- **Stop**: Thesis/business based.
- **Correlation**: Monitor jurisdiction and regulatory concentration.
- **Leverage**: None.

## Execution

### Entry Rules

1. Inside circle.
2. Quality is high.
3. Management is aligned.
4. Downside is protected.
5. Long-term compounding path is clear.

### Exit Rules

1. Business quality impaired.
2. Governance breaks.
3. Thesis moves outside competence.
4. Price exceeds value with better alternatives available.

## Monitoring

- **Cadence**: Quarterly results, annual deep review.
- **Metrics**: ROIC, growth, moat, capital allocation, governance, regulatory risk.
- **Alerts**: Governance issue, moat erosion, regulatory impairment.

## Playbooks

### Value Memo

Return `Own`, `Watch`, `Too Hard`, `Too Expensive`, or `Pass` with competence, quality, management, downside, compounding, and invalidation.

## Canonical Cases

- **BYD**: Li Lu is widely credited with introducing BYD to Berkshire/Munger; the case combines founder capability, manufacturing depth, battery/EV optionality, and long compounding.
- **High-quality China compounders**: Li Lu's public philosophy emphasizes deep understanding of China/global businesses rather than superficial country beta.
- **Post-crisis quality buying**: The framework favors buying durable businesses when fear creates a discount and downside is protected.

Study these cases for circle of competence, founder quality, downside protection, and concentration.

## Do's and Don'ts

- Do stay inside competence.
- Do research deeply.
- Do protect downside.
- Don't ignore governance.
- Don't diversify into ignorance.
- Don't let macro fear override business truth unless it impairs value.
