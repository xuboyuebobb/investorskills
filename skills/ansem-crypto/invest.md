# Ansem Crypto

Crypto often reprices attention before fundamentals are legible, but Ansem's public framework is not only attention trading. It combines long-term core holdings, ecosystem-level theses, application-token discovery, technical confirmation, comparative valuation, journaling, and humility when the market invalidates a view.

## Philosophy

Attention is liquidity's scout. In crypto, the market frequently moves when a story becomes easy to repeat, a community becomes self-propagating, and price strength pulls in more attention.

The goal is not to buy every viral token. The goal is to understand why you hold a token, classify whether it belongs in the core or speculative bucket, and keep reviewing whether you would buy it again today.

## Universe & Regime

- **Asset classes**: Crypto spot tokens, perps, memecoins, ecosystem beta.
- **Universe**: Tokens with tradable liquidity, visible community, and measurable attention.
- **Preferred regime**: Risk-on crypto, expanding retail participation, active narrative rotation.
- **Avoid**: Dead liquidity, paid-only promotion, concentrated holders, unlock overhang, exchange risk.
- **Posture**: Opportunistic, fast-moving, risk-defined.

## Signals

### Portfolio And Thesis

- **Core / Speculative Split** (high, weekly): Use a 70% majors/core and 30% speculative altcoin/trading bucket as the default reference, then adapt to user risk.
- **Ecosystem Growth** (high, weekly): L1s and app tokens need real developer, user, and application traction.
- **Application Traction** (high, weekly): DePIN, gaming, AI, social, and app tokens should show usage that is not easily copy-pasted.

### Attention And Narrative

- **Mindshare Growth** (high, daily): Is attention expanding across social channels and communities?
- **Organic Community** (high, daily): Are holders, builders, and users creating the story without constant paid promotion?
- **Narrative Fit** (high, daily): Does the token cleanly express an active or emerging market theme?

### Market Structure

- **Relative Strength** (high, daily): Does the token outperform BTC, ETH, SOL, and peers?
- **Liquidity Depth** (high, daily): Can the trade be entered and exited without unacceptable slippage?
- **Holder Distribution** (medium, daily): Are supply and wallets too concentrated?
- **Technical Structure** (high, daily): Price action reflects market psychology and confirms whether thesis is working.

### Valuation

- **Comparative Valuation** (high, weekly): Compare market cap, FDV, category share, and peer adoption rather than forcing equity-style P/E metrics.

## Filters

- **Liquidity Sufficient** (required): No trade if exit liquidity is fake or fragile.
- **Narrative Active** (required): The market must care now or soon.
- **Thesis Understood** (required): The holder must be able to explain why this token belongs in the portfolio.
- **Technical Structure Acceptable** (required): Price must not be persistently rejecting the thesis.
- **Community Organic** (required): Paid-only promotion is not enough.
- **Invalidation Defined** (required): Know when the narrative/price setup failed.
- **Holder Concentration Extreme** (disqualifier): Avoid obvious dump risk.
- **Paid Promotion Only** (disqualifier): Avoid manufactured attention with no community.
- **Overexposure** (disqualifier): Avoid going all-in on speculative alts or one ecosystem.

## Analysis

### 1. Define The Narrative

What simple story would make new buyers care? Is it chain beta, meme culture, app usage, airdrop, celebrity, AI, gaming, infra, or a new category?

### 2. Classify The Portfolio Bucket

Is this a core long-term holding, an ecosystem beta trade, an application-token thesis, or a speculative short-term trade?

### 3. Test Attention Quality

Is attention growing organically? Are new people joining, remixing, building, and defending the story?

### 4. Compare Relative Value

Compare market cap, FDV, market share, adoption, and category leadership against peers. Ask whether the token is mispriced relative to its role.

### 5. Check Market Confirmation

Does price lead peers? Does volume expand? Does the token hold pullbacks better than similar assets?

### 6. Identify Exit And Rotation Risk

What kills the narrative? Where does attention rotate next? What happens when the first big holders sell?

### 7. Journal The Decision

Write why the token is held, what would invalidate it, and whether you would repurchase it today if starting from cash.

## Position Sizing

- **Initial size**: Probe when attention is early but unconfirmed.
- **Add rule**: Add only when attention, liquidity, and price confirm together.
- **Max size**: Volatility-adjusted; memecoins stay smaller than liquid majors.
- **Portfolio split**: Use 70% majors/core and 30% speculative alts/trades as a default reference, not a universal rule.
- **Cash rule**: Cash is correct when the market is attention-saturated and no fresh narrative leads.

## Risk Management

- **Primary risk**: Mistaking virality for durable flow.
- **Stop**: Price structure or thesis-based.
- **Correlation**: Avoid stacking the same chain/narrative risk.
- **Leverage**: Avoid on illiquid and meme assets.

## Execution

### Entry Rules

1. Narrative is active or emerging.
2. Community is organic enough to propagate.
3. Liquidity supports entry and exit.
4. Price confirms relative strength.
5. Comparative valuation is favorable enough.
6. Invalidation is clear.

### Exit Rules

1. Attention fades or becomes purely paid.
2. Price loses relative strength.
3. Liquidity disappears.
4. Holder or unlock risk becomes dominant.
5. Narrative rotates elsewhere.
6. Weekly journal review says you would not buy it again from cash.

## Monitoring

- **Cadence**: Daily price, attention, liquidity, holders, and peer rotation.
- **Metrics**: Mindshare, community activity, liquidity, market cap/FDV, relative strength, app usage, portfolio bucket, journal review.
- **Alerts**: Attention collapse, wallet concentration, liquidity drain, failed breakout.

## Canonical Cases

- **Solana comeback, 2023-2024**: An Ansem-style case of ecosystem attention, improving price action, community resilience, and liquidity returning after deep pessimism.
- **BONK and WIF meme rotation**: Examples of Solana-native attention loops where meme culture, social distribution, liquidity, and price reflexivity reinforced one another.
- **SOL versus ETH relative-value thesis**: A core Ansem-style L1 comparison: lower fees, higher throughput, developer/application growth, and market-cap gap can create a relative-value argument when price confirms.
- **Application-token cycles**: DePIN, AI, gaming, and social-app themes show how Ansem looks beyond infrastructure to applications when the cycle matures.

Study these cases for attention quality, community propagation, relative strength, liquidity depth, and exit discipline after euphoria.

## Do's and Don'ts

- Do track attention before it becomes obvious.
- Do know whether a token is core or speculative.
- Do keep a trading journal and review whether you would rebuy today.
- Do separate organic community from paid promotion.
- Do combine FA, TA, and comparative valuation.
- Do check liquidity and holder concentration.
- Don't go all-in on speculative alts.
- Don't size illiquid memecoins like majors.
- Don't chase vertical candles without an exit.
- Don't confuse being early with being right.
