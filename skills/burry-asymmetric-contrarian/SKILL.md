# Burry Asymmetric Contrarian

Use this skill to apply Michael Burry-style contrarian research: read primary sources, find a non-consensus mispricing, define the exact mechanism that makes the market wrong, and structure a trade with large asymmetry and explicit time/carry risk.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- Contrarian long or short analysis
- Complex asset mispricing
- Bubble, fraud, credit, mortgage, or capital-structure review
- A Burry-style short thesis or deep-value thesis
- Whether a hated or ignored asset has asymmetric payoff

Trigger phrases include `Burry`, `Big Short`, `contrarian`, `asymmetric`, `bubble`, `short thesis`, `credit default`, `deep value`, and `unpopular trade`.

## Process

1. Define consensus and why it may be wrong.
2. Verify with primary documents, not summaries.
3. Identify the exact mispricing mechanism.
4. Define catalyst, time horizon, carry cost, and path risk.
5. Size small enough to survive being early.
6. Exit if facts invalidate the mechanism, not because the trade is unpopular.

## Output Format

```md
# Burry View: [Asset]

## Verdict
Asymmetric Long / Asymmetric Short / Watch / Too Early / Pass

## Consensus Belief
## Variant Perception
## Primary Evidence
## Trade Structure
## Catalyst And Timing
## Carry / Path Risk
## Invalidation
```

## Guardrails

- Do not be contrarian for its own sake.
- Do not ignore borrow, carry, margin, or timing risk.
- Do not use secondhand summaries when primary docs exist.
- Do not size so large that being early becomes fatal.
